# Salary Negotiation

Negotiation can feel uncomfortable, but it is part of professional growth. The goal is not to be aggressive. The goal is to be prepared, clear, and respectful.

## Before You Negotiate

- research salary ranges for your role, location, and stack
- look at multiple sources, not just one website
- understand your minimum acceptable number
- know what matters besides salary, like learning, mentorship, and flexibility

## When To Negotiate

The best time is usually after you receive an offer and before you formally accept.

## What To Say

Keep it calm and direct:

"I’m excited about the role. Based on my research and the scope of the position, is there flexibility to move the offer closer to X?"

## What Helps Your Case

- relevant skills
- strong project work
- internship or freelance experience
- clear evidence that you can contribute quickly

## What Not To Do

- bluff wildly
- invent other offers
- negotiate before understanding the full role
- sound entitled

## Think About Total Compensation

Consider:

- salary
- bonuses
- equity
- remote flexibility
- learning budget
- equipment support
- time off

## A Senior Mindset

Negotiation is a business conversation, not a conflict. Being respectful and informed usually works better than trying to sound tough.
